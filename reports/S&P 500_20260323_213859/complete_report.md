**1. The "Golden Pocket" Fallacy**
The debate confirms that "convergence" is not always bullish. The Aggressive Analyst𠏋 reliance on the "lack of supply" is dangerous. In a ceiling trap, a lack of supply means there are no buyers. We must continue to trust the **Conservative Analyst𠏋** view that "lack of volatility is a trap."

**2. Respect the Ceiling**
The Trader𠏋 past history of "selling at the top" to secure a win was actually a strength, not a weakness. The new plan respects this by taking partial profits at the resistance, rather than trying to be a hero and hold for a theoretical infinite upside.

**3. Sector Rotation**
As the Trader learned, market-cap weighting is leverage. By scaling out, we are effectively rotating capital out of the over-leveraged mega-caps (the Ferrari) into cash or defensive assets, reducing our exposure to the very sector-specific volatility the Bear Analyst warned about.
