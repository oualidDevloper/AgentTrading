To suggest that a 10% pullback is merely a "buying opportunity" while ignoring the potential for a 40% or 50% drawdown in a highly volatile tech sector is reckless for our firm's balance sheet.

As competitors like AMD and the open-source community erode the ecosystem's lock-in, the pricing power they rely on could evaporate overnight. We cannot assume the "secular trend" is irreversible simply because the company is currently profitable; history is littered with profitable companies that were eventually disrupted.

Finally, the argument that "profitable infrastructure beats hype" is misleading. High growth rates often require massive reinvestment, which can mask underlying cash flow weakness or burn through reserves. A conservative strategy isn't about waiting for the "bottom," it's about ensuring we enter the trade with a margin of safety. We should not buy into this momentum at current levels; instead, we should wait for a meaningful correction to secure a lower cost basis and mitigate the risk of a sharp, immediate reversal.
